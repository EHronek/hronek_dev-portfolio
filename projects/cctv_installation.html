CCTV & NVR Infrastructure Deployment – Rehema Hospice and Clinic

  • Role: Technical Lead / IT Systems Coordinator
  • Team Size: Led a team of 3 technicians
  • Environment: 24/7 operational healthcare facility
  • Technologies: IP Cameras, NVR, PoE, Ethernet Networking, VLANs, Access Control

Led the complete end-to-end deployment of a hospital-wide CCTV and NVR surveillance system, from initial planning and system design to installation, configuration, testing, and final handover. The project was executed in a mission-critical healthcare environment, requiring high reliability, strong security controls, and minimal disruption to daily hospital operations.

I acted as the overall technical lead, coordinating both the technical execution and the team workflow. This involved working closely with hospital management and security personnel to identify high-risk zones, define surveillance requirements, and ensure compliance with internal security policies.

Key Responsibilities & Contributions:

  • Conducted a comprehensive site survey and risk assessment across wards, entrances, corridors, and parking areas.
  • Designed camera placement to eliminate blind spots while optimizing coverage and performance.
  • Planned and implemented structured Ethernet cabling and PoE connectivity.
  • Installed and configured NVR hardware, storage, and recording policies.
  • Configured camera streams, motion detection, alerts, and recording schedules.
  • Implemented system security best practices (credential hardening, access control, network isolation).
  • Performed end-to-end system testing and performance optimization.

Beyond technical deployment, I ensured the system was operationally sustainable. I prepared technical documentation and delivered hands-on training to hospital security and IT staff, covering system monitoring, footage retrieval, basic troubleshooting, and escalation procedures.

Impact & Results:

  • Delivered a fully operational, secure, and scalable CCTV surveillance system.
  • Improved hospital-wide visibility and incident response capabilities.
  • Reduced security blind spots in high-risk and high-traffic areas.
  • Enabled hospital staff to independently manage and maintain the system.

This project demonstrates my ability to lead real-world IT infrastructure deployments, work effectively in high-pressure environments, and bridge the gap between hardware, networking, security, and user support — skills directly applicable to IT Support, Systems Administration, Network Engineering, and Infrastructure-focused roles.
